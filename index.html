About the Festival

What is Onam?

Onam is Kerala's most beloved harvest festival, a ten-day celebration rooted in the legend of King Mahabali, whose reign is remembered as a golden age of prosperity, equality, and joy. Each year, Keralites around the world welcome his return with flowers, feasting, and festivity.

MASC Upstate brings this tradition to Greenville, South Carolina, creating a space where the Malayalee community and their friends and neighbors can experience the warmth of Kerala culture, share a traditional Onam Sadya, and celebrate together as a family.

The Experience

What to Expect

Cultural Performances

An evening of vibrant Kerala art forms bringing the spirit of the festival to life on stage.

Traditional Sadya

A grand Onam feast served on a banana leaf, with over twenty traditional Kerala dishes.

Thiruvathira

A graceful circle dance performed by women in traditional Kerala attire, an enduring symbol of Onam.

Pookalam

Intricate floral rangoli patterns crafted from fresh flower petals, a hallmark of every Onam celebration.

Family Activities

Fun and engaging activities for guests of all ages, celebrating the inclusive spirit of Onam.

Community Fellowship

A gathering of the Malayalee community in Upstate South Carolina, and all who wish to share in the celebration.
